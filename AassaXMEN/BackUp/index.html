Welcome to Physics at EAB!



Physics is the most fundamental of sciences, as it aims to provide answers about the nature of the building blocks of the Universe, what they are and how they behave. Research in Physics has laid the ground work for the development of most technologies and continues to be a driving force in shaping what our everyday lives look like.


IB Y1
The IB diploma is a demanding course and requires a set of procedures to ensure your success. This is a two-year, rigorous course with an extensive syllabus and involves learning and using most of the basic physical concepts. The first year of the course covers topics in Mechanics, Wave Phenomena, and Thermodynamics.
IB Y2
This course covers topics in Energy Production, Astrophysics, Fields, Electromagnetism, Structure of Matter, Quantum Physics, and Nuclear Physics.
High School Physics
The course is an introduction to the study of the constitution of matter and its behavior. The approach to this High School Science one-year course is led by the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS).
High School Design
The High School Design course aims to familiarize the student with the design and engineering process used broadly all over the world. Students will go through the nine steps of design to produce bridges, impact resistant vehicles, games, computer programs and websites. By the end of the semester, they will undertake a project of their own choosing that solves a problem in the world or improves upon an existing solution.
Quantum Studies Club
A club for students with a deeply ingrained need to know the fundamental inner workings of the universe. Students will study the evolution of physics from the late 19th century to the present day, understanding the challenges classical mechanics faced and how quantum mechanics came to be, culminating in the standard model. At the same time, a modern introduction to the math behind the standard model will slowly alloww the students to understand and find simple solutions to Schrodingers equations.
Coding Club
Coding Club is an exciting opportunity for students to learn about basics of coding! In Coding Club students learn many different languages of coding such as JavaScript and HTML. Which can be further used to develop their own websites, apps and programs in future. In Coding Club we will also work on group projects like building game or websites together once we get used to coding. No prior knowledge on Coding is required!
e-NABLE
e-NABLE is a group of individuals from all over the world who use 3D printers to create free 3D printed hands for those in need of an upper limb assistive device. e-NABLE at the American School of Brasilia (EAB) uses the 3D printers on campus to print parts of the hand. Once the printing it done, students assemble the hand, using flexible and rigid chords and wires, as to distribute the 3D printed hands to people in need of them in Brasilia.
STEM Club
STEM Club is for the people who want to learn more about Math and Science. Or for the people who are struggling with their Math or Science classes and need help. STEM Club is also for the people who want help others learn. STEM is a supportive and exciting community where students can work together to help and support each other in improving their understanding in Math and science.
